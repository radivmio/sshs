Key Terms

Class Days
Total days allocated for teaching and learning from the start of Term 1 to the end of Term 3.
Wellness Break
A scheduled, protected period within the End-of-Term Block for physical, mental, social, and emotional well-being. Academic tasks and formal instruction are suspended.
Instructional Block
A designated period of class days devoted to teaching and learning across all approved learning delivery modalities.
Term Examination
The summative assessment administered during the last week of the Instructional Block in each term.

Learning Continuity in Emergencies

Guidelines on Learning Continuity in Emergencies — the Hayo-Hinto Framework

"Teacher and learner well-being is foundational to education."

HAYO
Continue
In-Person Classroom Learning
Ideal — learners & teachers physically safe and ready
  • Regular in-person teaching continues
  • Full lesson guides, full curriculum delivery
  • Remediation programs delivered
  • Periodical exams on schedule; no class days missed
HINAY
Ease-In
Structured Distance Learning
Minor to moderate disruptions; mild stress or uncertainty
  • Learning slowed intentionally to reduce pressure
  • Online asynchronous, digital modules, print modules, learning packets
  • Formative assessments limited to core competencies
  • Half class days missed; exams rescheduled; no work immersion
HINGA
Check-In
Light Distance Learning
Heightened stress due to emergency impact; academic demands reduced
  • Focus on physical, psychological & social-emotional well-being
  • Broadcast materials, check-in guides, simplified learning resources
  • Formative assessments optional and low-stakes
  • Half class day missed; exams rescheduled; no work immersion
HINTO
Stop
All Learning Suspended
Safety and basic needs at risk; displacement highly likely
  • Academic learning completely halted
  • Full attention to protection, emergency response, basic needs
  • Resources focused on psychological safety and well-being
  • Full class days missed; exams rescheduled; no work immersion

Assessment, Grading & Recognition

Revised Guidelines on Classroom Assessment, Grading System, and Awards and Recognition for the K–12 Basic Education Program

Summative Assessment Components

✍️
Written / Oral Works
WW

Essays, journals, written reports, structured presentations. Tasks that scaffold and contribute to PTs.

Recommended: 3–5 per learning area, per term
🎯
Product / Performance Tasks
PT

Projects, portfolios, investigations, culminating presentations. Emphasize integration of concepts and higher-order thinking.

Recommended: 2–3 per learning area, per term
📋
Examinations
EX

Summative Tests (STs) and Term Examinations (TE). ST shall not exceed half of the total items in the TE.

Recommended: 2 STs + 1 TE per term — TE: 60 items

Ethical Use of AI in Assessment

🔍
Authenticity of Evidence

Outputs shall reflect the learner's own work, thinking, and performance.

🎯
Alignment with Learning Intent

AI use shall not compromise intended learning outcomes.

📢
Transparency and Disclosure

Permitted AI use shall be explicitly declared.

⚖️
Equity and Access

Assessment shall not disadvantage learners due to unequal access to AI tools.

Term Examinations and other supervised assessments shall be AI-prohibited, except when authorized for accessibility or approved purposes.
